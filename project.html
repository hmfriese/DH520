A Brief Explanation of WAR

Wins Above Replacement (WAR) is "...an attempt by the sabermetric baseball community to summarize a player's total contributions to their team in one statistic" (Slowinski, 2010). WAR acts as an easy statistic that encapsulates what a player contributes to their team. WAR is calculated using different metrics for pitchers and position players but each are attempts to place a numbered value on any given player. In theory, if WAR measures how many wins a player will contribute to their team then annual player salary should rise alongside WAR.

A Note on Player Salaries

In the MLB there are three phases of player salary negotiations, all of which are dependent on a player's Service Time. The first is League Minimum, where players are signed to short-term, low value contracts. For the time-span considered here the league minimum was $535,000-$555,000 (MLB Player's Association, 2017). League Minimum covers the first three years of a player's Major League career. Once a player has accrued three years of Service Time they become arbitration eligible where they may negotiate their salary with their team. Arbritration typically covers years 3-6 of service time for a player. A player under arbitration is only permitted to negotiate salaries and contracts with the team that they already have a contract with (MLB Player's Association, 2017). There is no league maximum salary during arbitration. However, theoretically by the time a player reaches arbitration their net value to their team should have increased with experience and therefore they should be offered a higher salary. Once a player has reached six years of Service Time they become eligible for free agency where they are free to negotiate salary and contracts with any team across the Major Leagues (Brown, 2016).

It should be noted that this is a highly simplified explanation of how player salaries are negotiated and determined. In practice the salary negotiation process is highly regulated by the MLB Collective Bargaining Agreement and features many minutiae.
